# Hetchins Hellenic Spyder

 **Manufacturer:** Hetchins (United Kingdom)  
 **Production Period:** c. late 1960s–1990s (custom-built, limited production)  
 **Category:** Hand-built steel road racing bicycle

## Overview

The **Hetchins Hellenic Spyder** represents one of the most technically unusual and visually distinctive bicycles produced during the golden age of British framebuilding. Combining the patented **Hellenic rear triangle design** with Hetchins' renowned craftsmanship, the model was developed for riders seeking maximum frame rigidity, ride quality, and exclusivity.

The defining characteristic is the Hellenic stay arrangement, originally designed by Fred Hellens, where the seat stays extend forward beyond the seat tube and connect beneath the top tube. This configuration increases structural triangulation and reduces flex under load while creating a highly recognizable silhouette.

## Technical Specifications

| Feature | Specification |
|----------|-------------|
| Frame Material | Reynolds 531, Reynolds 653, or custom steel tubing |
| Construction | Lugged steel |
| Bottom Bracket | English threaded |
| Fork | Lugged steel fork |
| Dropouts | Campagnolo or forged steel |
| Brake Type | Side-pull caliper |
| Wheel Size | 700C |
| Typical Groupsets | Campagnolo Nuovo Record, Super Record |

## Distinctive Features

- Hellenic stay configuration
- Hand-cut ornate lugs
- Extensive chrome plating
- Custom geometry options
- Low-volume artisan production
- British lightweight bicycle heritage

## Historical Significance

The Hellenic Spyder is among the most collectible Hetchins bicycles due to its rarity and engineering uniqueness. Surviving examples are frequently found in specialist vintage bicycle collections and museums.

# Peugeot PX-10

 **Manufacturer:** Peugeot Cycles (France)  
 **Production Period:** c. 1963–1983  
 **Category:** Professional road racing bicycle

## Overview

The **Peugeot PX-10** was the flagship racing model of Peugeot and one of the most successful French racing bicycles ever produced. Widely used by professional teams competing in the **Tour de France**, the PX-10 became synonymous with French cycling excellence during the 1960s and 1970s.

Constructed from full **Reynolds 531 double-butted tubing**, the PX-10 offered exceptional strength-to-weight ratio and was considered among the finest steel racing frames available.

## Technical Specifications

| Feature | Specification |
|----------|-------------|
| Tubing | Reynolds 531 Double Butted |
| Fork | Reynolds 531 |
| Weight | Approximately 9–10 kg complete |
| Geometry | Professional racing |
| Brakes | Mafac Racer |
| Derailleurs | Simplex Prestige / Campagnolo |
| Crankset | Stronglight |
| Wheels | Tubular racing wheels |

## Distinctive Features

- Full Reynolds 531 construction
- Nervex Professional lugs
- Chrome fork crowns
- Lightweight racing geometry
- Professional racing pedigree

## Historical Significance

The PX-10 served as Peugeot's premier racing platform and helped establish the company's international reputation in professional cycling.

# Peugeot Carbolite 103

 **Manufacturer:** Peugeot Cycles (France)  
 **Production Period:** Late 1970s–1990s  
 **Category:** Recreational road bicycle

## Overview

**Carbolite 103** refers to Peugeot's proprietary high-tensile steel tubing specification widely used on entry-level and mid-range bicycles. Although often mistaken for a model name, it primarily identifies the frame material.

The tubing was designed to provide durability, affordability, and easy maintenance while preserving the ride characteristics associated with steel bicycles.

# Colnago Oval CX Gentleman

 **Manufacturer:** Colnago (Italy)  
 **Production Period:** Mid-1980s–early 1990s

## Overview

The **Colnago Oval CX Gentleman** was derived from the racing-oriented Oval CX platform but incorporated more relaxed geometry intended for endurance riding and gran fondo events.

The frame utilized **ovalized Columbus steel tubing**, which improved lateral stiffness and pedaling efficiency while maintaining the smooth ride quality expected from premium steel.

- Chromed fork crown
- Campagnolo component compatibility
- Precision Italian craftsmanship
