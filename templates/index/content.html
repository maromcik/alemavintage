Our Bicycles

Restoring vintage bicycles is more than just a hobby – it’s an art and a tribute to the history of cycling. This process combines craftsmanship, technical expertise, and a love for detail. Bikes from brands like Colnago, among the most iconic in the cycling world, require a particularly sensitive approach to preserve their historical value and aesthetic elegance.

1. Project Start: Assessing the Bike’s Condition

The first step in restoration is a thorough inspection of the bicycle. Every detail – from the frame and components to the original paintwork – is analyzed to determine the level of damage and restoration possibilities. For older Colnago models, it’s crucial to identify whether the bike features original components, such as Campagnolo shifters, brakes, or cranks.

This is where a photo of the bike’s original condition, showing worn paint, rust, or damaged components, would be fitting.

2. Dismantling the Bike

After assessment, the bike is dismantled. All components are carefully removed and sorted into parts suitable for restoration, repair, or replacement. Every piece, from the smallest screws to the frame, is meticulously cleaned to remove dirt, grease, and corrosion.

A photo from this stage could show the bike disassembled on a workbench, with its parts organized for restoration.

3. Restoring the Frame

The frame is the heart of any bike and is often the most prominent feature of older Colnago models. The restoration process includes:

  • Removing the original paint, if damaged, usually through sandblasting or chemical cleaning.
  • Repairing rust, cracks, or damaged joints.
  • Applying new paint, often in colors and styles that match the original. This includes hand-painted Colnago logos and decals, typical for the model.

In some projects, special modifications, such as modernizing the colors, may be chosen but always with respect for the original design.

A photo of the restored frame would beautifully illustrate this phase.

4. Restoring and Replacing Components

Functional components are carefully cleaned and lubricated. Damaged or worn parts, such as chains, gears, or brakes, are replaced with authentic pieces, ideally from the same era. If that’s not possible, modern equivalents are used, maintaining the bike’s aesthetic integrity.

5. Assembling the Bike

After restoring the frame and components, the bike is reassembled. This step requires precision to ensure all parts fit perfectly and the bike is functional and safe. This includes adjusting the gears, brakes, and chain tension.

A photo of the assembled bike ready for final adjustments would be perfect.

6. Final Tuning and Testing

The final step is testing the bike to ensure everything works correctly and that the bike provides the riding comfort that matches its original quality. Every detail, from smooth shifting to optimal tire pressure, is carefully checked.

A photo of the finished bike in all its glory could be the final touch.

Result: Restored Beauty

A restored Colnago bike is more than just a machine – it’s a piece of history brought back to life. Each reconstructed model is unique, combining meticulous craftsmanship, respect for heritage, and a passion for cycling.

Restoring vintage bicycles is challenging, but the result is worth it. A restored Colnago shines with its beauty once more and can serve not only as a functional bicycle but also as a decorative piece or a symbol of a passion for cycling.

Historical Bicycles and the Legend of Colnago

Heritage and Elegance

Historical bicycles are more than means of transportation – they reflect engineering skill, cultural shifts, and artistic value. These machines, developed since the early 19th century, carry the stories of their time on their frames. From simple wooden bikes with solid wheels to sophisticated steel frames of the 70s and 80s – every era brought advancements that shaped cycling.

In the 20th century, especially between 1970 and 1990, cycling became not just a sport but a lifestyle. Bikes from this period are valued for their combination of craftsmanship and functionality. Steel frames, hand-brazed joints, unique geometries, and innovative components turned vintage bicycles into treasures that still hold high value today.

Bikes from this era were used not only for racing but also for recreational cycling. Brands like Bianchi, Peugeot, and Colnago were industry leaders, each bringing something unique. The most notable was the Italian brand Colnago, whose bold innovations and aesthetic sense influenced the entire cycling world.

About the Colnago Brand

Founded in 1954 by Ernesto Colnago in Cambiago, Colnago is an icon of Italian design and innovation. The brand is renowned for combining traditional craftsmanship with cutting-edge technology, creating bikes that are both functional and beautiful.

Ernesto Colnago began his career as a mechanic and quickly realized that bicycles could be not just tools for winning but also works of art. His goal was to create bikes that pushed performance boundaries while maintaining timeless aesthetics. This vision led to the creation of one of the most respected brands in cycling.

Technological Innovations

Colnago is known for its pioneering technologies. In the 1980s, the brand introduced the use of carbon fiber, revolutionizing bicycle manufacturing. The first carbon frame model, the Colnago C40, became legendary in classic races like Paris-Roubaix.

The same innovative approach can be seen in steel models like the Colnago Master. This bike featured unique Gilco Design tubing and the Precisa straight fork, improving stiffness and handling. Additionally, Colnago experimented with aerodynamic shapes, internal cable routing, and unique braking systems.

Success in Professional Cycling

Colnago has been an integral part of the professional peloton. Bikes from this brand helped riders like Eddy Merckx, Giuseppe Saronni, and Johan Museeuw achieve victories in the world’s most prestigious races, including the Tour de France and Giro d’Italia.

Models like the Colnago Super and Colnago Mexico were favorites among racers for their lightness and agility. Colnago, however, never rested on its laurels, continuously innovating to meet the demands of modern racing.

Iconic Models

Among the most famous Colnago models are:

  • Colnago Super: A classic from the 70s that defined racing bicycles for over a decade.
  • Colnago Mexico: Designed in honor of Eddy Merckx and his hour record, it was lighter and stiffer than its predecessors.
  • Colnago Master: The pinnacle of steel technology, with innovative tubing and a straight fork, ideal for professional races.

Legacy and Future

Colnago is more than just a bicycle brand – it’s a symbol of passion, craftsmanship, and engineering precision. Every bike is the result of dozens of hours of handwork and embodies the best of Italian design.

Even today, more than 60 years after its founding, Colnago remains at the forefront of the cycling world. Their modern models, like the Colnago C68, combine advanced technologies with heritage, appealing to a new generation of cyclists. And for those seeking a piece of history, older Colnago models are still highly sought after by collectors and classic bike enthusiasts.
